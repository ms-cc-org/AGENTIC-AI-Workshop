# Campus IRB Policy Guideline: Generative AI and Agentic Tools in Human Subjects Research (SYNTHETIC DATA)

> **Scope:** This policy applies to all university investigators, research staff, and student researchers utilizing Generative AI (GenAI), Large Language Models (LLMs), or autonomous agentic tools to process, analyze, or interact with human subjects data.

---

## 1. Data Classification and Vendor Compliance

Processing human subjects data with AI tools is strictly governed by the identifiability of the dataset and the compliance status of the vendor.

* **Identifiable Private Information (IPI):**
* **Prohibition:** Investigators **must not** input, upload, or process IPI using any generative AI or agentic tool unless a formal **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** is active with the vendor *and* the specific protocol has been explicitly approved by the IRB.


* **De-identified Data:**
* **Requirement:** De-identified human subjects data may only be processed using **institutionally approved vendors** that guarantee data privacy and prevent external training.


* **Free/Consumer AI Services:**
* **Strict Prohibition:** Free, consumer-grade AI services (e.g., standard public search, free writing assistants, or public chat models) **are not approved** for any regulated, sensitive, or human subjects research data.



---

## 2. Mandatory Documentation and Audit Trails

To ensure scientific reproducibility, transparency, and regulatory compliance, investigators are required to maintain a comprehensive audit trail of all AI-assisted data processing. For every study protocol involving AI, you must document and retain:

1. **Model Specifications:** The exact model name, API endpoint (if applicable), and specific version number (e.g., `GPT-4o (version 2024-05-13)`).
2. **Prompt Registry:** A complete, chronological log of all system prompts, user queries, and structured templates used to process the data.
3. **Raw Outputs:** The exact, unaltered outputs generated by the AI tool prior to any human editing.
4. **Retention Period:** These records must be retained alongside the primary research data for the standard institutional audit period (typically 3 to 7 years post-study closure).

---

## 3. Risk Assessment: Passive Assistants vs. Autonomous Agents

The IRB classifies conversational and agentic AI systems into two distinct risk tiers based on their level of autonomy. Autonomous execution presents a significantly higher risk profile.

| Feature | Tier 1: Passive Assistants | Tier 2: Autonomous Agents |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Definition** | Tools used to analyze data, draft text, or summarize transcripts under direct human guidance. | Systems configured to execute actions, make decisions, or interact with participants independently. |
| **Examples** | AI transcription services, qualitative coding assistants, draft-generation tools. | Automated messaging bots, self-scheduling agents, adaptive intervention systems. |
| **Human-in-the-Loop** | **Required.** All outputs must be thoroughly reviewed by a researcher before integration. | **Strictly Required.** No autonomous action or decision that affects a participant may occur without real-time human sign-off. |
| **IRB Risk Level** | **Low to Moderate** | **High** (Requires full committee review in most cases) |

---

*Note: Non-compliance with these guidelines may result in a formal finding of protocol deviation, suspension of research activities, and the potential invalidation of collected research data.*
