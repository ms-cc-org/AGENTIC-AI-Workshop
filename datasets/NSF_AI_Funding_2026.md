## 1. Program Overview & Objectives

This solicitation supports visionary projects that apply **agentic Artificial Intelligence (AI)** and **Machine Learning (ML)** pipelines to revolutionize university-level cyberinfrastructure. The program aims to automate, secure, and scale:

* **Research Data Management (RDM):** Intelligent metadata generation, automated data curation, and policy-compliant repository routing.
* **Scientific Reproducibility:** Agentic workflows that construct self-documenting, auditable, and executable computational pipelines.
* **Advanced Cyberinfrastructure:** AI-driven optimization of high-performance computing (HPC) resource allocation and distributed sensor network management.

## 2. Award Information & Budget Limits

The program expects to fund projects that demonstrate high-impact, scalable, and institution-agnostic cyberinfrastructure solutions.

* **Maximum Award Amount:** Up to **$600,000** (inclusive of direct and indirect costs).
* **Project Duration:** Up to **3 years** (36 months).
* **Cost Sharing:** Not required (and strictly prohibited under standard NSF policy unless explicitly mandated).
* **Anticipated Funding Portfolio:** Approximately 10–15 active awards, subject to the availability of federal funds.

## 3. Eligibility & Proposal Limits

To ensure broad institutional participation and equitable distribution of resources, strict eligibility and submission limits apply.

### Institutional & PI Requirements

* **Eligible Institutions:** U.S. Institutions of Higher Education (including community colleges, accredited universities, and minority-serving institutions).
* **Principal Investigator (PI) Eligibility:** The PI and co-PIs must hold a full-time, tenure-track faculty or research-staff appointment at the submitting institution at the time of proposal submission.
* **Institutional Submission Limit:** A maximum of **two (2) proposals** may be submitted per unique institution as the lead organization. There is no limit on participation as a sub-awardee or non-lead collaborator.

## 5. Proposal Preparation & Special Review Criteria

Proposals must be prepared in accordance with the current *NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide* (PAPPG) and must address the following specialized criteria:

### I. Intellectual Merit & Broader Impacts

Beyond the standard NSF merit review criteria, proposals must explicitly address how the proposed agentic tool enhances the efficiency of the broader academic community, with an emphasis on lowering technical barriers for non-traditional or under-resourced research institutions.

### II. Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP)

In alignment with 2026 PAPPG updates (Supplement 2), the proposal’s DMSP must not be submitted as a traditional PDF attachment.

* **Format:** PIs must complete the structured **DMSP Webform** directly inside Research.gov.
* **Required Disclosure:** The webform must detail the precise training datasets, model weights (if proprietary/custom), and API usage paths associated with any proposed agentic workflows.
* **Data Sharing Timeline:** Pursuant to 2026 guidelines, all data supporting publications must be publicly deposited in an approved repository *at the time of publication*.

### III. Special Requirement: Reproducibility Statement

All proposals must include a dedicated **Reproducibility Statement** (limited to 2 pages, uploaded under "Supplementary Documents"). This statement must explicitly answer:

> **The Portability Test:** How can the scientific results, metadata, or data pipelines generated by your AI agent be fully replicated, verified, and audited by an independent researcher who *does not* have access to your specific proprietary model, API keys, or specialized agentic runtime?
> PIs are encouraged to detail their fallback deterministic pipelines, open-source model alternatives (e.g., Llama-based local deployments), and comprehensive prompt/system logging frameworks.
